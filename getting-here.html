The Last Few Steps

Finding Our Entrance

A nine-step picture walkthrough — from the street to your room

We send your keybox code via WhatsApp (or email) 24 hours before your arrival. The walkthrough below shows what you'll see at each step.

Step 1 · The Street
Walk down Kralja Tomislava

Our building is the one straight ahead, at the end of the street on the right. At street level look for our small gold sign-board on the gate.

Step 2 · The Building
Look for the ATM
Our entrance

You'll see our stone building with the ATM at street level. The narrow stone staircase running up its side leads to our gate.

Step 3 · Number 12
The stone staircase

At number 12, right next to the ATM, you'll find a small stone staircase. Walk up to the white gate at the top.

Step 4 · The Gate
Oblivion Rooms · 4th floor

A small white gate with our gold Oblivion Rooms sign. The gate isn't locked — there's a small metal slide bolt on the inside; reach through the bars and slide it open, then push the gate through.

Step 5 · Up the Inner Stairs
A few more steps to the door

Through the gate, climb the narrow stone stairs up to the wooden door at the top.

Step 6 · The Keybox
Mounted on the wall, right of the door

On the wall just to the right of the wooden door — a small keybox with our OBLIVION LUXURY ROOMS plate. Enter the code we sent you.

Step 7 · Your Key
Two keys inside — take yours

Inside: two keys. Take the RED key for Room I — The Roman Red, or the BLUE key for Room II — The Paris Blue. Both rooms are on the top floor.

Step 8 · Top Floor
The Oblivion door

Climb four floors to the top. You'll find our wooden door with the Oblivion Luxury Rooms plate. Your one key opens everything you need — the building entrance, this top-floor door and your own room. By design it will not open the other guest's room; please don't try it, so the other guests stay undisturbed.

Step 9 · Welcome Home
Your room number is on the door

Inside our small lobby — your room number is on the door. Coffee-table guides, a welcome desk, and an iPad with everything you need to know about Split. Welcome to Oblivion.

Where to Leave the Car

Parking

Important

Stopping or parking in front of our building — even for a few seconds — is strictly forbidden. The street is covered by traffic cameras, and any vehicle left at the door will receive an immediate fine. There are no exceptions.

Need to drop off luggage? You can briefly stop your car around Park Đardin (a one-minute walk from our door) to disembark bags before parking elsewhere. Stopping in front of our building remains strictly forbidden.

We do not offer on-site parking. Diocletian's Palace has no private parking lots inside its walls — only public parking in the surrounding streets. Below are the closest options.

Driving

By Car

Our address is not accessible by car — the Old Town is a pedestrian zone by city regulation. Drive into Split, park nearby (see Parking above), and walk the final few minutes.

By Air

From Split Airport (SPU)

Approximately 25 km · 25–40 min from the Old Town

✦
Our Private Shuttle — The Easy Way
A pre-arranged transfer with our trusted driver, picking you up directly at airport arrivals. €40 paid to the driver. Let us know your flight details when booking your stay so we can have someone waiting for you.
✦
Official Airport Shuttle Bus — The Cheap Way
Departs 20 minutes after each flight, dropping at the central station in the city centre — a 5–10 minute walk to our rooms. Fare is €6–8 per person. The most reliable budget option.
✦
Uber & Bolt — Best App-Based Way
App-based rides are our recommended option in town — transparent pricing in the app, no negotiation, easy payment. From the airport: typically €30–50 depending on demand. The best balance of price and convenience for most arrivals.
✦
Local Taxis — Last Resort
Public taxis at the airport rank are not recommended. Prices range from €40 to €100 and vary widely between drivers. Always agree the price before getting in — this is essential.
By Land or Sea

From Bus, Train & Ferry Stations

Split's main bus station, train station and ferry port all sit right next to each other on the eastern edge of the Old Town — a short, scenic walk from our door.

✦
From the Bus Station — 10 min walk
Walk along the harbour toward the Riva, follow the Palace's southern wall, then turn left into the Old Town. We're a few steps further along Kralja Tomislava.
✦
From the Ferry Port — 8 min walk
Arriving by ferry from Hvar, Brač, Vis, Šolta, Korčula or Italy? Step off the boat and walk along the Riva — Oblivion is just around the eastern end of the Palace walls.
✦
From the Train Station — 10 min walk
The train station sits beside the bus station and ferry port. Same walk along the Riva — pleasant in any weather.
